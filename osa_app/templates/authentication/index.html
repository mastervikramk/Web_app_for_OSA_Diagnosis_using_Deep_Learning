Sleep Apnea

Sleep apnea is a sleep disorder characterized by pauses in breathing or periods of shallow breathing during sleep. It can have various health implications and often goes undiagnosed.

Types of Sleep Apnea

  1. Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA):

    2. Obstructive sleep apnea is the most common type. It occurs when the muscles in the throat relax excessively, leading to a blockage of the airway. This blockage can result in pauses in breathing or shallow breathing during sleep.

  2. Central Sleep Apnea (CSA):

    3. Central sleep apnea is less common and involves a failure of the brain to send the appropriate signals to the muscles to breathe. Unlike OSA, there is no physical obstruction in the airway.

  3. Complex Sleep Apnea Syndrome (Treatment-Emergent Central Sleep Apnea):

    4. Complex sleep apnea syndrome, also known as treatment-emergent central sleep apnea, occurs when a person initially diagnosed with OSA later develops symptoms of central sleep apnea, often as a result of continuous positive airway pressure (CPAP) therapy for OSA.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of sleep apnea include:

  • Loud snoring
  • Episodes of breathing cessation followed by gasping or choking sounds
  • Excessive daytime sleepiness
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Irritability
